So, we were talking about having an OOC 'leader' for our next foray into the dungeon--do we want to try this? Another thought was to have two leaders, one for dungeon delving and one for combat. As an alternative, it might be good if we came up with some general principles for dungeon delving, and some basic agreements for combat. For example, some principles for dungeon delving might include: -Mess with almost everything (if there's a lever in a box on the wall, pull the lever unless something indicates we shouldn't) -Leave the unknown alone (if there's a lever in a box on the wall, don't pull it unless we have some idea about what it might do) -Try to visit every room -Quit once we've accumulated [x]. -Don't fight the lich. -If at any time we want to change one of our adopted principles, that's fine. Some agreements for combat might include: -Reach consensus at the beginning of each round as to what the party's general strategy is going to be. Individual tactics are then left to the discretion of the players. -We'll try to retreat if the party is reduced to [x]% of its collective hit points and/or members. -TPK or 0PK--none of this 'escaping with 2/3 the party and never coming back to finish the adventure' stuff. If we have an actual 'leader' in either or both areas, those are the sorts of decisions I think I'd want them to make--but if we talk about it beforehand, we might not need one. Or we could have a 'moderator' person whose job it was to say, "OK, enough talk, time to vote". Or we could just say that anyone could 'call for a vote' if the discussion was circling too much. At any rate, I think at least thinking about these things beforehand could speed up play later. Any comments? Any opinions about the principles above?

"Rosie" wrote:
Mess with almost everything (if there's a lever in a box on the wall, pull the lever unless something indicates we shouldn't) Leave the unknown alone (if there's a lever in a box on the wall, don't pull it unless we have some idea about what it might do)
I think something in between these two alternatives would be good. Mess with everything, but do it carefully, and prioritize. We should do everything we can think of to prepare for any likely traps the lever is likely to spring on us, and we shouldn't do especially risky things when we're low on HP (or otherwise in bad shape).
Quote:
Try to visit every room
The IF player in me likes this idea. Barring, e.g., the room full of really huge monsters and lava. So, y'know, that prioritizing again. (We can always come back to those rooms after we've finished everything else in the dungeon, right?)
Quote:
Quit once we've accumulated [x] .
Pfft. No way. If we're going to end the module, why not end it seeing as much stuff as we can? No point in stopping playing unless either we win or we're all dead.
Quote:
Don't fight the lich.
This, on the other hand, seems reasonable. Possibly once we get far enough for this to matter (ha ha ha), we'll have some ideas for how to "win" without confronting the big baddie face to face.
Quote:
Reach consensus at the beginning of each round as to what the party's general strategy is going to be. Individual tactics are then left to the discretion of the players.
This seems reasonable, except for the fact that "reaching consensus" is the thing that we're especially bad at.
Quote:

How about "We'll officially consider a 'retreat' plan if the party is reduced to [...]"? As in, if things reach a certain point of badness, we don't continue until we've formally decided together that yes, we want to keep going, or no, we want to get out of here.
Quote:
TPK or 0PK--none of this 'escaping with 2/3 the party and never coming back to finish the adventure' stuff.
I fully agree with this, though. I'm fine with 'escaping with 2/3 the party and coming back later to finish the adventure', but if we're never going to return anyway, why not never return because we're dead, rather than never returning just because we're not idiots? There's no fun in being sensible. (Of course, 'never return because most of the players are sick of this deathtrap and have no interest in rolling up new characters' is an alternative, but hopefully one we can try to avoid.)
